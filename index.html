About Me

I am a avid UX designer who can not only create wireframes and prototypes but can solve problems of human experience by integrating my knowledge of cognitive psychology and ability to code. I am a part ux designer and part ux developer. As a ux designer, I understand the cognitive science behind every user actions and design an engaging interaction for them. As a ux developer, I develop the design into an actual interaction in a way that users can experience it in real world.

Projects

SEE Tab app

SEETab is an android tablet application used by a team of researchers at ThinkLab (UT Dallas) to study child cognition. This game like research method was traditionally conducted using powerpoint, however since that method had practical difficulties and also because children nowadays are tech savvy, the research team decided to conduct such research study on a tablet.
In this game like application, kids of ages 3-5 will see a set of images(Animals, birds, cars, etc.) and they can choose any one image to seek information on that particular item based on their curiosity.

Barrel Race

This android game application project aimed to recreate the traditional Barrel Race competition of the famous rodeo event. In this multiplayer game a horse rider attempts to complete a clover-leaf pattern around preset barrels in the fastest time. For maneuvering the horse rider around the clover shaped pattern of barrels, android device’s accelerometer sensor was used such that tilting the device moves the player’s horse in the game.

Easy Review Prototype App

EasyReview is a prototype app that I built to encourage people to change their behavior towards restaurant review/rating system. I observed a behavioral pattern among my friends where each person would look at the reviews before visiting a restaurant but almost never write a review in return after their visit even for extreme good or bad reviews which would benefit others.

Skype UI Redesign

This project is redesign of Skype’s mobile interface. I and my team partner decided upon redesigning skype because we felt that skype’s interface was very complex and confusing. Particularly, we observed distressed and overwhelmed older adults during our user research, while they tried to navigate through skype’s convoluted interface.
